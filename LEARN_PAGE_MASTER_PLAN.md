## Executive Summary

The `/learn` page has the potential to become a category-defining mental health education resource. The current foundation is strong — comprehensive content, warm tone, faith-informed perspective without being preachy. To become the best, we need to:

1. **SEO**: Implement schema markup, create topic-specific URLs, optimize for featured snippets
2. **Backlinks**: Partner with universities, churches, healthcare providers, and grief organizations
3. **UX**: Add crisis-first design, reduce cognitive load, enable personalization
4. **Trust**: Add medical review, citations, transparency about AI assistance
5. **Conversion**: Create clear pathways from learning to support

**Differentiator**: Most mental health resources are either clinical (NIMH, WebMD) or advocacy-focused (NAMI). HelpGuide comes closest to our warm, accessible approach but lacks the faith perspective. Our unique position: *AI-powered research accessibility + faith-informed compassion + lived-experience foundation*.

## Current State Analysis

### What's Working

- **Comprehensive content**: Covers conditions, medications, natural approaches, family support, careers, crisis
- **Warm, honest tone**: "Mental illness lies" — this is powerful, human writing
- **Faith integration done right**: Present but not pushy, respectful of all readers
- **Good structure**: Sidebar navigation, clear sections
- **Crisis-aware**: Banner at top, crisis section included

### What Needs Improvement

#### SEO Issues
- [ ] Single URL for all content — no way to rank for specific conditions
- [ ] No schema markup (MedicalWebPage, FAQPage, etc.)
- [ ] No internal linking strategy
- [ ] Missing canonical URLs and hreflang

- [ ] Missing FAQ structured data for featured snippets

- [ ] No medical review disclaimer with named reviewer
- [ ] No citations or references to studies
- [ ] No author information or credentials
- [ ] No "last updated" date visible

#### Conversion Issues
- [ ] No email capture within content
- [ ] No "next steps" after reading sections
- [ ] No connection to chatbot or other tools
- [ ] No pathway to community/support

## SEO Strategy

### 1. URL Structure Overhaul

**Current**: `/learn` (all content on one page)

**Proposed**: Topic-based URL structure

```
/learn                              → Hub page (overview + navigation)
/learn/conditions                   → Conditions hub
/learn/conditions/depression        → Individual condition
/learn/conditions/anxiety
/learn/conditions/ptsd
/learn/conditions/bipolar
/learn/conditions/ocd
/learn/conditions/adhd
/learn/medications                  → Medications hub
/learn/medications/ssris
/learn/medications/snris
/learn/medications/[etc]
/learn/natural-approaches           → Natural hub
/learn/natural-approaches/exercise
/learn/natural-approaches/sleep
/learn/natural-approaches/[etc]
/learn/for-family                   → Family guide
/learn/careers                      → Careers in mental health
```

**Benefits**:
- Each page can rank for specific keywords
- Better internal linking
- Improved user navigation and bookmarking
- More targeted meta tags and schema

### 3. Schema Markup Implementation

```json
// Example: Depression page schema
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "MedicalWebPage",
  "name": "Depression: Symptoms, Causes, and Treatment",
  "description": "A comprehensive guide to understanding depression...",
  "mainContentOfPage": {
    "@type": "MedicalCondition",
    "name": "Major Depressive Disorder",
    "alternateName": ["Depression", "Clinical Depression"],
    "signOrSymptom": [
      {"@type": "MedicalSignOrSymptom", "name": "Persistent sadness"},
      {"@type": "MedicalSignOrSymptom", "name": "Loss of interest"}
    ],
    "possibleTreatment": [
      {"@type": "MedicalTherapy", "name": "Cognitive Behavioral Therapy"},
      {"@type": "Drug", "name": "SSRIs"}
    ]
  },
  "reviewedBy": {
    "@type": "Person",
    "name": "[Medical Reviewer Name]",
    "credential": "MD, Psychiatry"
  },
  "dateModified": "2026-04-21",
  "lastReviewed": "2026-04-21",
  "audience": {
    "@type": "PeopleAudience",
    "healthCondition": {
      "@type": "MedicalCondition",
      "name": "Depression"
    }
  }
}
```

**Schema types to implement**:
- `MedicalWebPage` — All learn pages
- `MedicalCondition` — Condition pages
- `Drug` — Medication pages
- `FAQPage` — FAQ sections
- `BreadcrumbList` — Navigation
- `Organization` — Foundation info
- `Article` — For blog-style content

### 4. Featured Snippet Optimization

Target "People Also Ask" and featured snippets with structured Q&A:

```markdown
## What is depression?

Depression is more than sadness. It's a persistent flattening of mood
and energy that affects how you think, feel, and handle daily activities.
Unlike normal grief or disappointment, clinical depression lasts for
at least two weeks and significantly impairs daily functioning.

## What causes depression?

Depression rarely has a single cause. It typically involves a combination of:
- **Genetics**: Family history increases risk
- **Brain chemistry**: Imbalances in neurotransmitters
- **Life events**: Trauma, loss, chronic stress
- **Medical conditions**: Thyroid disorders, chronic illness
- **Medications**: Some drugs have depression as a side effect
```

## User Experience for People in Need

### Core UX Principles

1. **Crisis-first**: Anyone in crisis should find help immediately
2. **Reduce cognitive load**: People with depression/anxiety have limited bandwidth
3. **Calm, not clinical**: Warm colors, readable fonts, white space
4. **No dead ends**: Every page leads somewhere helpful
5. **Respect autonomy**: Inform, don't prescribe

### Crisis Design Implementation

#### Always-Visible Crisis Access

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ [!] In crisis? Call/text 988 | Text HOME to 741741 | Chat  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- Fixed position on mobile
- Doesn't scroll away
- High contrast but not alarming
- Direct links (tel:, sms:)

#### Crisis Detection & Soft Intervention

When users navigate to suicide, self-harm, or crisis-related content:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                              │
│  Before you continue, we want to make sure you're okay.     │
│                                                              │
│  If you're having thoughts of suicide or self-harm,         │
│  please reach out now:                                       │
│                                                              │
│  [📞 Call 988]  [💬 Text 988]  [💻 Chat Now]                │
│                                                              │
│  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────  │
│                                                              │
│  [Continue to article]          [I'm researching for others]│
│                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Cognitive Load Reduction

#### Reading Time & Progress

- Show estimated reading time per section
- Progress indicator for long pages
- "Save for later" functionality (localStorage)

#### Typography for Mental Health

- Minimum 18px body text
- 1.6-1.8 line height
- Max 70 characters per line
- High contrast (WCAG AAA)
- Sans-serif for body, limited use of serif

#### Chunked Information

- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences max)
- Bullet points for lists
- One idea per section
- Clear headings every 200-300 words
- Summary boxes for key takeaways

### Personalization Features

#### "Your Journey" Onboarding

On first visit:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                              │
│  What brings you here today?                                 │
│                                                              │
│  [I'm struggling myself]                                     │
│  [I'm supporting someone]                                    │
│  [I'm a professional/student]                                │
│  [Just learning]                                             │
│                                                              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

This personalizes:
- Content recommendations
- Tone of CTAs
- Support resources shown
- Related content suggestions

#### Bookmarking & Progress

- "Save this article" (heart icon)
- "Mark as read" for sections
- "Return to where you left off"
- Downloadable PDFs for offline reading

### Accessibility Requirements

#### WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

- [ ] Skip to main content link
- [ ] Keyboard navigation for all interactive elements
- [ ] Screen reader-optimized headings
- [ ] Alt text for all images
- [ ] Captions for any video content
- [ ] Color contrast minimum 4.5:1
- [ ] Focus indicators visible
- [ ] No flashing content
- [ ] Resizable text up to 200%

#### Mental Health-Specific Accessibility

- [ ] Calm color palette (avoid harsh reds except crisis)
- [ ] Minimal animations (respect prefers-reduced-motion)
- [ ] No auto-playing media
- [ ] Clear exit paths (never trapped)
- [ ] Gentle error messages
- [ ] No countdown timers or urgency tactics

## Content Strategy

### Content Pillars

1. **Understand** — What conditions are, how they work
2. **Treat** — Medications, therapy, natural approaches
3. **Cope** — Daily strategies, self-care, resilience
4. **Support** — Helping others, family resources
5. **Hope** — Recovery stories, research advances, faith perspective

### Content Types to Add

#### 1. Expert Contributions (Builds authority)

- Invite psychiatrists, therapists, pastoral counselors to review/contribute
- Add "Reviewed by [Name], [Credentials]" to each page
- Consider a professional advisory board

#### 2. Research Digests (Differentiator)

- Monthly summaries of mental health research
- "What the latest studies say about [condition]"
- Ties to our AI research mission

#### 3. Personal Stories (Builds trust)

- "Voices of Recovery" section
- User-submitted stories (moderated)
- Family perspectives
- Video testimonials (optional)

#### 4. Practical Tools (Drives engagement)

- Downloadable safety plan template
- Medication tracking worksheet
- Sleep hygiene checklist
- Questions to ask your doctor
- Symptom tracker (printable)

#### 5. Faith & Mental Health Series (Unique angle)

- "What Does the Bible Say About..." series
- How churches can support mental health
- When to pray AND when to get professional help
- Addressing "just pray harder" theology

### Content Quality Standards

#### Every Page Must Have:

- [ ] Last updated date
- [ ] Medical review attribution (when applicable)
- [ ] Estimated reading time
- [ ] Crisis resources link
- [ ] Next steps / related content
- [ ] Sources / references
- [ ] Social sharing options
- [ ] Print / PDF option

#### Writing Guidelines:

- **Reading level**: 8th grade (use Hemingway Editor)
- **Tone**: Warm, direct, hopeful without toxic positivity
- **Pronouns**: "You" for reader, "they/them" for examples
- **Avoid**: Jargon, scare tactics, false promises
- **Include**: Actionable next steps, validation, hope
