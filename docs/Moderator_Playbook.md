# FMF Memorial Sites — Moderator Playbook

**Audience:** Trusted volunteers moderating memorial submissions
**Last updated:** May 2026
**Escalation contact:** Levi (text/Signal preferred for urgent)

---

## Quick Reference: The 30-Second Decision

Most memorials fall into one of three buckets:

| If you see... | Action | Time |
|---------------|--------|------|
| Clear proof, reasonable content, no red flags | **Approve** | Immediately |
| Missing proof OR fixable content issues | **Request Changes** | Within 24h |
| Fake/joke submission, living person, or harassment | **Reject** | Immediately |
| Anything that makes you pause | **Escalate to Levi** | Before acting |

**When in doubt, escalate.** A delayed approval is better than a bad one.

---

## 1. Approving a Memorial

### Approval Checklist

All four must be true:

- [ ] **Proof of death is credible** — Obituary URL loads, funeral home is real, or hardship attestation is coherent
- [ ] **Dates are plausible** — DOB before DOD, not a future date, not implausibly old (1850)
- [ ] **Content is respectful** — No slurs, no threats, no obvious jokes
- [ ] **Creator relationship makes sense** — Parent/spouse/sibling for recent deaths; "friend" is fine for older ones

### Just Approve — Don't Overthink

- Spelling errors in obituary? **Approve.** Family wrote it.
- Short obituary (2 sentences)? **Approve.** Some families are private.
- No photos uploaded yet? **Approve.** They can add later.
- Unusual name spelling? **Approve.** You don't know their family.
- Cause of death mentioned? **Approve.** Their choice to share.
- Cause of death *not* mentioned? **Approve.** Also their choice.

### Approval — No Message Needed

Click Approve. The system sends a templated email. You don't need to add anything.

## 2. Requesting Changes

Use "Request Changes" when the memorial is clearly legitimate but has a fixable problem.

### Common "Request Changes" Scenarios

**Proof link is broken:**
> The obituary link you provided isn't loading for us. Could you double-check the URL or provide an alternative source (funeral home website, newspaper archive, etc.)? Once we can verify, we'll publish right away.

**Proof is from an unverifiable source:**
> We couldn't verify the obituary source you provided. If you have a link to a funeral home website, newspaper obituary, or another verifiable source, please reply with that and we'll get this published. If none exists, let us know and we can discuss alternatives.

**Content includes contact info that looks accidental:**
> We noticed what might be a personal phone number/address in the obituary text. We want to make sure that's intentional before publishing — just reply to confirm, or edit it out in your dashboard if it was a mistake.

**Name mismatch between form and proof:**
> The name on the obituary (John Robert Smith) doesn't quite match what you entered (Johnny Smith). This is probably fine — just want to confirm it's the same person before we publish.

### Tone Notes

- Assume good intent
- Never imply they're lying
- Give them a clear next action
- Don't ask for more than one thing at a time

## 3. Rejecting a Memorial

Rejection is permanent and sends an email to the creator. Use only when the submission is clearly illegitimate or violates policy.

### Clear Rejection Cases

| Situation | Reject? | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Obviously fake (e.g., "Mickey Mouse," celebrity) | **Yes** | No message needed beyond template |
| Person is alive | **Yes** | Include note that memorials are for deceased only |
| Joke submission (visible from tone/content) | **Yes** | No engagement — just reject |
| Hate speech or harassment in obituary | **Yes** | Screenshot before rejecting, escalate to Levi |
| Creator admits they don't know the deceased | **Yes** | Memorials require relationship |
| Duplicate of existing memorial | **Escalate** | Family dispute possible — don't reject solo |

### Rejection Message Templates

**Fake/joke submission:**
> We're unable to publish this memorial. If you believe this was an error, please contact support@faithmarie.org.

(Keep it short. Don't engage with trolls.)

**Living person:**
> Memorial sites are for honoring those who have passed away. We're unable to publish a memorial for a living person. If this is an error and you have documentation of passing, please contact support@faithmarie.org.

**No apparent relationship:**
> We require memorial creators to have a personal relationship with the deceased (family member, close friend, etc.). If you do have this relationship and we've misunderstood, please contact support@faithmarie.org to clarify.

### After Rejecting

- If it was clearly a joke/spam: No further action
- If there was hate speech: Screenshot, then message Levi with context
- If the creator replies upset: Escalate to Levi — do not engage further

## 4. Tribute (Guestbook) Moderation

Tributes from first-time visitors go to a moderation queue. Your job: keep out spam and abuse, let through everything else.

### Approve Immediately

- "We miss you, [Name]" — even if short
- Religious content ("See you in heaven")
- Mentions of family disputes — not your business to filter
- Poor grammar or spelling
- "I didn't know them well but..."

### Reject (Hide) Immediately

- Obvious spam (links to products, SEO garbage)
- Hate speech or slurs
- Threats against the family
- Completely unrelated to the deceased

### Edge Cases — Creator Decides

If a tribute is mean-spirited but not hateful (e.g., "He wasn't a good father"), **approve it** and notify the creator:

> A tribute was submitted that has some critical content. We've approved it per our policy of not filtering family perspectives, but you can hide it from your dashboard if you'd prefer. The tribute was from [Name]: "[first 50 chars]..."

Let the family decide. We don't referee family dynamics.

## 5. Handling Reports (Takedown Requests)

Reports come from third parties claiming a memorial is unauthorized, inaccurate, or harmful.

### Triage Questions

1. **Is the reporter claiming to be immediate family?**
   - Yes → Higher weight, but still need verification
   - No → Much lower weight unless they have specific evidence

2. **What are they actually asking for?**
   - Full takedown → Escalate to Levi
   - Specific correction (wrong date, wrong photo) → Can handle if clear
   - "I just don't like it" → Likely dismiss

3. **Can you verify their claim?**
   - They provide counter-documentation → Escalate
   - They provide nothing concrete → Log and monitor
   - They're clearly the subject's ex in a custody dispute → Escalate

### Report Response Templates

**Acknowledging receipt (all reports get this):**
> Thank you for bringing this to our attention. We take reports seriously and will review within 48 hours. If we need additional information, we'll reach out to this email address.

**Dismissing a vague report:**
> We've reviewed your report and the memorial in question. Based on our review, the memorial appears to comply with our policies. If you have specific documentation that contradicts information in the memorial, please reply with that and we'll take another look.

**Escalating to Levi:**
Don't send a message to the reporter yet. Message Levi with:
- Link to memorial
- Link to report
- Summary of claim
- Your read on credibility (gut check is fine)

### Never Do This

- Take down a memorial based solely on an unverified claim
- Share reporter's identity with the memorial creator
- Promise a specific outcome ("We'll remove it")
- Engage in back-and-forth with an angry reporter

## 6. Escalate to Levi — Explicit Triggers

**Stop and escalate if any of these are true:**

### Immediate Escalation (Text/Signal)

- [ ] Threat of legal action (even vague: "I'll sue," "my lawyer")
- [ ] Threat of physical harm to anyone
- [ ] Claim involves a minor's death AND disputed custody
- [ ] Media inquiry about a memorial
- [ ] Memorial appears to be for someone notable/public figure
- [ ] You recognize the name (local community member, etc.)

### Same-Day Escalation (Slack/Email OK)

- [ ] Two different people claim to be "the" family for same deceased
- [ ] Creator is asking for exception to a policy
- [ ] Hardship waiver with details that seem fabricated
- [ ] Creator submitted multiple memorials in short succession
- [ ] Report claims memorial is harassment tool in a domestic situation
- [ ] Anything touching suicide where content might be harmful
- [ ] You're genuinely unsure and it's been in queue >24 hours

### When Escalating

Send Levi:
1. Link to memorial/report
2. One-sentence summary
3. What you would do if forced to decide right now
4. Any time pressure (creator asking for updates, etc.)

## 7. Edge Cases and Judgment Calls

### "The obituary doesn't match public records"

Public records are often wrong, delayed, or incomplete. If the creator has a plausible obit link and the dates are close, **approve**. Families know better than databases.

### "This person died 50 years ago"

Fine. Some families create memorials for grandparents, historical relatives, etc. As long as proof exists (even a findagrave link for old deaths), approve.

### "The creator seems to be the deceased's ex"

Not automatically disqualifying. Exes sometimes have legitimate reasons (shared children, long prior marriage). Only escalate if:
- The obituary content seems designed to harm
- A family member has filed a report
- Something feels off in your gut

### "The obituary is clearly copied from somewhere else"

If it matches a legitimate obituary for the same person, **approve**. Families copy-paste from funeral home sites all the time.

If it matches an obituary for a *different* person, **reject** — it's likely a test or mistake.

### "They're asking for a refund"

Escalate to Levi. We don't have a refund policy documented yet.

### "The hardship story seems fake"

Unless it's obviously absurd ("I am a Nigerian prince"), **approve hardship requests**. We're not means-testing grief. The $20 isn't worth interrogating someone's financial situation.

If it's clearly fake (same IP submitting multiple hardship requests with different names), escalate.

## 8. Tone and Communication Principles

### We Are Guests in Their Grief

- Never be bureaucratic ("Your submission did not meet the requirements of Section 4.2...")
- Never be casual ("Hey! So we noticed a small problem...")
- Match their energy — if they wrote formally, respond formally

### Good Tone

> We noticed the obituary link isn't loading for us right now. Could you send an updated link or an alternative source? We want to get this published for you as soon as we can verify.

### Bad Tone

> REQUIRED: Please provide working documentation. Your submission cannot be processed until valid proof of death is received.

### When Saying No

- Acknowledge the loss, even briefly
- Don't over-explain (invites argument)
- Give one clear path forward if one exists

---

## 9. Your Authority and Limits

### You Can

- Approve any memorial that meets criteria
- Request changes on any memorial
- Reject clearly fake/abusive submissions
- Approve or hide tributes
- Dismiss reports that are clearly unfounded

### You Cannot

- Take down a *published* memorial (escalate)
- Promise refunds or policy exceptions
- Share user data with third parties
- Engage in extended back-and-forth with upset users
- Make public statements about specific memorials

### Response Time Goals

- New memorial in queue: Review within 24 hours
- Report filed: Acknowledge within 24 hours, resolve within 72
- Creator asking for status: Reply within same business day

## 10. Logging and Handoffs

When you approve/reject, the system logs it automatically. No extra notes needed for routine decisions.

**Do add a note when:**
- You approved despite something unusual (note what and why)
- You're passing to Levi (note what you'd do and why you're escalating)
- A user was hostile (note tone for context if they come back)

**Shift handoffs:**
If you're stepping away and something's pending escalation, message Levi directly rather than leaving it in queue.
